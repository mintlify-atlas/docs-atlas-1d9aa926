## Overview

Asimov v0 is a complete bipedal leg system for humanoid robots, featuring 12 degrees of freedom (6 DOF per leg) with an articulated toe design. The mechanical structure is built with off-the-shelf components and is fully compatible with low-volume manufacturing processes.

<Note>
  The design utilizes an RSU (Revolute Spherical Universal) ankle mechanism that provides robust ankle actuation through a coupled motor system.
</Note>

## Naming Conventions

All mechanical components follow a standardized naming convention to ensure clarity across documentation, simulation, and hardware:

![Naming Convention](/images/naming_convention.png)

The naming convention uses consistent prefixes:
- **L_** prefix for left leg joints
- **R_** prefix for right leg joints
- Descriptive suffixes indicate joint type: **_Pitch**, **_Roll**, **_Yaw**

## MJF 3D Printing Compatibility

All mechanical components are designed to be compatible with Multi Jet Fusion (MJF) 3D printing technology, enabling rapid prototyping and low-volume manufacturing:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Material Selection">
    MJF-compatible parts can be printed in PA12 (Nylon 12) or PA11 materials, providing excellent mechanical properties and durability.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Design Tolerances">
    All clearances and fits are designed with MJF manufacturing tolerances in mind, ensuring proper assembly without post-processing.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Surface Finish">
    MJF parts have a slightly textured surface finish that provides good friction characteristics for assembled components.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Production Scaling">
    MJF technology allows for economical production of 10-1000 units, making it ideal for research labs and small-scale production.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key Design Features

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Articulated Toe" icon="shoe-prints">
    Provides better ground contact and push-off during walking, improving stability and efficiency.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="RSU Ankle Mechanism" icon="gears">
    Couples two motors to provide pitch and roll motion with reduced complexity compared to traditional designs.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Off-the-Shelf Motors" icon="motor">
    Uses standardized Encos motor models, ensuring availability and serviceability.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Modular Design" icon="puzzle-piece">
    Individual joints can be serviced or replaced without disassembling the entire leg structure.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
