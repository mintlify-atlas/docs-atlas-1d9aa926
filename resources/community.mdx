## How to Contribute

### Opening Issues

Found a bug or have a feature request? Open an issue on GitHub:

1. Check if a similar issue already exists
2. Provide a clear, descriptive title
3. Include relevant details:
   - Steps to reproduce (for bugs)
   - Expected vs. actual behavior
   - Your environment (OS, software versions, etc.)
   - Photos or screenshots if applicable

### Pull Request Guidelines

When submitting a pull request:

- **Describe the changes**: Explain what you've changed and why
- **Reference issues**: Link to related issue numbers
- **Keep it focused**: One PR should address one feature or fix
- **Test your changes**: Ensure mechanical designs are valid or code runs correctly
- **Follow naming conventions**: Use the established naming scheme for components
- **Update documentation**: Include relevant documentation updates

<Note>
  All contributions must comply with the CERN-OHL-S-2.0 license. By submitting a pull request, you agree to license your contributions under the same terms.
</Note>

## Community Guidelines

We're committed to maintaining a welcoming and inclusive community:

### Be Respectful
- Treat all community members with respect and kindness
- Welcome newcomers and help them get started
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences

### Be Collaborative
- Share knowledge freely and help others learn
- Give credit where credit is due
- Build on each other's ideas constructively

### Be Open
- Embrace the open-source spirit
- Share your work, including failures and lessons learned
- Accept feedback gracefully and provide it thoughtfully

### Stay On Topic
- Keep discussions relevant to Asimov and robotics
- Use appropriate channels in Discord
- Report spam or inappropriate behavior to moderators
